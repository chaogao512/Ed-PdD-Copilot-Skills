# Theory and Policy Checklist

- Does the paper translate policy into concrete research demand?
- Does the theory generate constructs, design principles, indicators or interpretation?
- Are core concepts defined consistently?
- Does the contribution go beyond applying a fashionable technology?
- Does the paper distinguish education value, governance value and technical feasibility?

## Anti-patterns

- Quoting policy documents without analytical use.
- Naming a theory in the introduction but never using it in method or discussion.
- Treating “digital transformation” as a self-evident good.

## Chinese Policy-Use Audit

When a manuscript cites Chinese education digitalization policy, check:

| Policy use | Acceptable use | Weak use |
|---|---|---|
| 教育信息化2.0 | Derives a specific digital governance requirement, such as resource integration, data governance or teacher capacity | Says “national policy supports this study” only |
| 教师数字素养 | Defines teacher competence dimensions and evidence requirements | Treats platform training hours as teacher competence |
| 教育强国建设规划纲要 | Connects digital education to quality, equity and governance modernization | Uses “education power” as a general slogan |
| 国家智慧教育平台 | Provides context for platformization and resource access | Claims platform construction itself proves governance improvement |

If policy is only decorative, mark at least MAJOR and require a “policy demand -> governance contradiction -> research design” rewrite.
