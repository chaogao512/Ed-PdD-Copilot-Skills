# Method and Evidence Checklist

- Are research questions answerable by the selected method?
- Is each claim matched to evidence of the same type?
- Are samples, instruments, data sources and analysis steps reported?
- Are reliability, validity, trustworthiness or consensus procedures appropriate?
- Are limitations tied to actual evidence weaknesses?

## Claim downgrade rules

- No control/comparison: avoid strong causal claims.
- No longitudinal data: avoid long-term transformation claims.
- Satisfaction only: avoid learning or governance effectiveness claims.
- Aggregate only: avoid equity and fairness claims.

## Causal-Claim Audit

Before accepting words such as “promotes”, “improves”, “enhances”, “causes” or “significantly increases”, check:

1. Is there a baseline or comparison condition?
2. Are groups equivalent or are confounds controlled?
3. Is the outcome a valid measure of the claimed construct?
4. Is the intervention implemented consistently enough to interpret?
5. Is attrition or missing data reported?
6. Are qualitative/process data available to explain how change occurred?

If these are missing, rewrite the claim as perceived usefulness, implementation feasibility, association, mechanism hypothesis or preliminary evidence.
