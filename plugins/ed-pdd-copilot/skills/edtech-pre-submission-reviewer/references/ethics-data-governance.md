# Ethics and Data Governance Checklist

Check:

- Informed consent or institutional authorization.
- Student and teacher data minimization.
- Anonymization or desensitization.
- Role-based access control.
- Human review for high-stakes recommendations.
- Explanation and appeal route.
- Fairness or subgroup risk check.
- Data retention and deletion plan.
- AI use disclosure when required.

## CRITICAL triggers

- Identifiable student data used without safeguards.
- Automated high-stakes decision with no human review.
- Sensitive profiling hidden from participants.

## Education AI and Data Governance Audit

Use this audit when a manuscript involves AI, learning analytics, student profiles, multimodal classroom data, teacher evaluation, smart campus dashboards or early-warning systems.

| Audit item | What to check | Severity if absent |
|---|---|---|
| Purpose limitation | The manuscript states why each data type is needed and what it will not be used for | MAJOR |
| Data minimization | The design avoids collecting extra video, audio, biometric or behavioral data | CRITICAL/MAJOR |
| Authorization and consent | Participants or institutions authorize data use in a documented way | CRITICAL |
| Role-based access | Sensitive data and AI outputs are not visible to unnecessary actors | MAJOR |
| Explainability | Affected users can understand the basis of important recommendations | MAJOR |
| Human oversight | Teachers, administrators or ethics committees review high-impact outputs | CRITICAL |
| Appeal and correction | Students or teachers can question, correct or contextualize results | MAJOR |
| Fairness check | The study checks subgroup risk and unequal impact | MAJOR |
| Retention and deletion | Data storage period and deletion procedure are stated | MAJOR |
| Disclosure of AI use | AI assistance in research or writing is disclosed when required | MAJOR |

## High-Risk Education Scenarios

Treat the following as high risk unless the manuscript proves safeguards:

- Student early warning, risk profiling or mental-health inference.
- Automated scoring that affects grades, admissions, awards or discipline.
- Teacher performance analytics used in appraisal.
- Classroom video/audio analytics with identifiable learners.
- Cross-department student data sharing for management decisions.

For high-risk scenarios, require a stronger burden of proof: human review, audit trail, explanation, appeal route, data minimization and institutional responsibility.
