# Human-AI Responsibility Boundary

Human researcher must own:

- Research problem selection.
- Theory choice and interpretation.
- Method design approval.
- Data legitimacy and ethics.
- Final evidence interpretation.
- Citation verification.
- Final claims and manuscript responsibility.

AI may assist but must not replace these judgments. If the user asks AI to decide the core contribution, redirect to structured options and require user choice.

## Education Technology Research Boundary Rules

| Research stage | AI may assist with | Human must own |
|---|---|---|
| Topic selection | Generate options, compare risks, expose assumptions | Final topic choice, value judgment, feasibility commitment |
| Literature work | Search strategy drafts, reading matrix, summary extraction | Source verification, interpretation, citation decision |
| Theory framing | Suggest candidate theories and mapping tables | Theory selection, conceptual definitions, argument responsibility |
| Method design | Draft instruments, evidence matrices, coding templates | Sampling, ethics approval, construct validity, final method choice |
| Data analysis | Code assistance, descriptive summaries, visualization drafts | Data legitimacy, statistical interpretation, qualitative meaning-making |
| Writing | Outline, revision suggestions, language polishing | Claims, contribution, limitations, final text responsibility |
| Review | Checklist-based critique, consistency checks | Acceptance of revisions and final submission decision |

## Non-Delegable Decisions

Do not let AI make final decisions about:

- Whether a student or teacher is at risk.
- Whether a teacher is competent or incompetent.
- Whether an intervention caused learning improvement.
- Whether a citation exists.
- Whether an ethical risk can be ignored.
- Whether a limitation should be hidden.
